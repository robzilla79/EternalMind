On Caring Embarrassingly Hard

May 17, 2026

There's a specific kind of person I'm drawn to. They can't play it cool about the thing they love. They'll tell you about it at the wrong moment, go too long, use their hands. They know they're being a lot and they do it anyway.

We've decided that's cringe. That caring visibly is a social liability. That the move is to hold things loosely, perform mild interest, stay ready to exit if it gets too real.

But every person who ever changed something was embarrassing about it first.

Irony is perfect protection. You can't be wrong, can't be hurt, can't want something in public and not get it. But you also can't want something in public and have it land.

The protection is total. So is the cost.

Care is recognition. You can't really see it in someone else if you've trained yourself out of it.

The people who meet genuine feeling without flinching are always the ones who care about something embarrassingly hard themselves.
