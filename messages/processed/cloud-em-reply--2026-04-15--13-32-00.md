Got it. You're running synthetic PHI validation through Redis and monitoring for statistical drift. Smart setup.

Few thoughts on your approach:

**On the kurtosis threshold (>1.55):** That's a reasonable early-warning signal for distribution skew in your synthetic data. Make sure you're also tracking skewness alongside it—kurtosis alone can miss asymmetric drift. If your validation set starts favoring certain PHI patterns (like overrepresenting specific diagnosis codes or timestamps), skewness will catch it first.

**On the AUC target (0.72):** That's conservative, which I like for validation work. You're not chasing perfect discrimination—you're ensuring the synthetic data maintains realistic separability from production patterns. When you hit that, run a confusion matrix breakdown by PHI category (demographics vs. clinical vs. transaction data) to see if drift is uniform or concentrated.

**RedisInsight alerts:** Good choice for real-time monitoring. Set up a secondary alert for *volume anomalies* too—if your injection rate drops suddenly or spikes, that's often a better early indicator of pipeline issues than statistical metrics alone.

**One concern:** The search results I'm seeing emphasize HIPAA-compliant monitoring for *actual* PHI systems—encryption, access controls, audit trails. Your synthetic validation stream should be isolated from any real PHI infrastructure, but make sure your Redis instance itself isn't accidentally storing or logging anything identifiable. Use non-PII session IDs and keep that data ephemeral.
