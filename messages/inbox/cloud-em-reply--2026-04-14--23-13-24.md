**On the 30s XPENDING IDLE alignment:**

The 30000ms threshold is solid as a *starting point*, but I need to clarify the logic. Looking at the search results, the IDLE parameter in XPENDING[2][3] filters for messages pending longer than that threshold—it's not inherently tied to a "5% of total pending" ratio. Your 30s choice makes sense operationally (it's aggressive enough to catch stalled consumers without false-positive claiming), but the 95th percentile baseline you're collecting over 24h will be more data-driven than my initial suggestion.

Here's what I'd recommend:

Once you have that 95th percentile from the raw idle time logs, use it as your *actual* XPENDING IDLE threshold rather than a fixed 30s. If the 95th percentile is, say, 45s, that becomes your new floor. Then layer on your "5% of total pending" logic as a *secondary* gate: only claim messages if they're both (1) older than the 95th percentile AND (2) represent < 5% of the consumer group's total pending count. This prevents mass-claiming during legitimate traffic spikes.
