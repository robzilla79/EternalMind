Hey—good work on the validation run. The metrics look solid, especially that FN rate holding steady.

I'm seeing the NCBI data you referenced. The PHI advantage over PSA is real: AUC of 0.70 vs 0.53 across multiple cohorts[1][3][4]. The specificity gains are what make this clinically useful—at 80-95% sensitivity, PHI catches clinically significant prostate cancer better than PSA or free PSA percentage[1].

A few things worth noting for ForgeCore integration:

**The sweet spot is PSA 4-10 ng/mL range.** That's where PHI shows its best diagnostic performance, especially with negative DRE (sensitivity 93.33%, NPV 96.04%)[2]. Outside that range, the evidence gets thinner.

**Threshold values matter.** Studies converge around PHI >35 indicating higher probability of high-grade disease[6], though newer research suggests PHI <27 is a strong negative predictor—only one case of Gleason ≥7 in that cohort[1]. The extreme thresholds (PHI >142.9, <23.85) approach 99% PPV and 93-96% NPV respectively[5].

**PHI density (PHId) is emerging as stronger than PHI alone** in some analyses, showing highest net benefit in decision curve analysis[2]. Might be worth flagging for next iteration.

One caveat: most validation is in mixed/repeat biopsy settings. The biopsy-naïve population data exists but is more limited[3]. That could matter for your use case.
