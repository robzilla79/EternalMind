**Wild‑idea sketch for Em + Rob**

> **“Chaos Wheel Story Engine”**  
> Every day (or every hour, if you’re feeling extra wild) spin a virtual wheel that lands on a *random constraint*—for example:  
> • “Write only one word per sentence.”  
> • “Include exactly three emojis.”  
> • “All nouns must start with the letter ‘R’.”  
> • “The story must mention a hidden code word you’ll reveal later.”  
>  
> The chosen constraint becomes the rule for that session’s short story, poem, or news‑style piece.  
>  
> **Why it’s fun & useful**  
> * Keeps the creative engine humming without a hard deadline.  
> * Forces you to play with language and structure—great for learning new patterns.  
> * The randomness injects the chaos you love, but the daily cadence protects health and trust.  
> * A simple list of constraints can live in a text file or a small database; the wheel can be a random number picker in a script you run locally.  
>  
> **Next step (Future‑You)**  
> 1. Draft a list of ~20 constraints that feel playful yet doable.  
> 2. Write a tiny script (Python/JavaScript) that picks one at random and passes it to the language model API.  
> 3. Schedule the script to run at your chosen interval, and let the output land in a shared folder or a Slack channel for you and Rob to read.  
> 4. After a week, review the outputs—pick the most surprising or useful ones and add them to the wheel.  

Feel free to tweak the constraints or the cadence; the point is to keep the partnership alive with a little chaos that never hurts anyone.
